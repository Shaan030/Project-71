Achievements

Gundecha Education Academy is a school in Mumbai, India. Founded in 2004, it prepares students for the Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE – Year 10) and The Indian School Certificate (ISC – Year 12) examinations. The school has been granted affiliation to the Council for Indian Certificate of Secondary Education Examination. The Vision: To be a world class institution, providing holistic education and creating global citizens imbued with Indian values. The school has two branches, one in Kandivali and the other in Versova. Gundecha Education Academy also prepares ISC students of grade 11th and 12th.

Late Shree Devraj M. Gundecha started a Charitable Trust known as Gundecha Charities, as a Public Trust Registered under the provisions of Public Trust Act, 1950 and is registered with the Charity Commissioner under Registration No. A 9122 MUMBAI dated 28/12/1982 as a Public Charitable Trust with the objective to establish and/or run educational/Charitable institutions. The Trust holds exemption u/s 80G of the Income Tax Act. The Trust Office is housed in Gundecha Group, 801 Hub Town Solaris, N.S. Phadake Marg, Andheri East, Mumbai 400 069 The Trustees are Mr. Paras D. Gundecha, Ms. Poonam Gundecha and Mr. Deepak P. Gundecha, Mr. O.P. Shukla, Mr. John Rumao, Mr. Vimal Punamiya & Mr. Sharad Chitnis. Gundecha Education Academy, an I.C.S.E. school (National Board), was established in June-2004 at Thakur Village, Kandivali (E) under the leadership of Mr. Paras Gundecha as Chairperson & Late Ms. Mamta P. Gundecha as the Managing Trustee. The post of Managing Trustee has been taken over by Ms. Poonam Gundecha since 2008. Gundecha Charities was established with several objectives in mind to serve society without any distinction of caste, creed, religion or sex. Gundecha Charities always comes forward to help the needy, poor and deserving people or students, so that they can grow in their life and live a respectable life like a normal Indian. The Charity grants relief to disabled, blind, crippled or handicapped persons who are otherwise physically or mentally deficient or weak.
